    You can slap some american cheese on some macaroni and call it "mac and cheese" and technically you'd be right...

    But was it good?

    For me, a lot factors into making a mac and cheese as tasty as it can get! You have to be asking the right questions like... were there breadcrums, making that perfect addition to the texture and taste? What was the noodle? I know macaroni makes the mac in mac and cheese, but now you can find that different curly noodles enter the classification of mac and cheese. And not all noodles are equal! What were the cheeses used? For me I'm a five cheeses kinda girly, so the more cheese the better! some other minor things to consider is presentation and price. No matter how good the mac is, if it's wallet-breaking it'll deffinately not hit the same!

    DISCLAMER: I am vegitarian! which means there was no meat in any of my ordes and would maybe change how'd you'd rank my list!
